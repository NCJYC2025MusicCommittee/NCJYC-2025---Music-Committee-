Praise & Worship Team Guidelines

“Serve the Lord with gladness; Come before His presence with singing.” — Psalm 100:2 NKJV

1. Heart of Worship

  • Let us always remember that our main purpose is to lead others into the presence of God, not to perform.
  • Each member is encouraged to come with a prayerful, humble, and prepared heart, ready to worship.
  • True worship begins long before we sing or play — it starts from a heart that honors and adores the Lord.
  • Our excellence in music is not for display, but for offering our best to God in worship.
  • We encourage every member to uphold a good testimony that honors God and reflects a life devoted to Him.

2. Punctuality

  • Please be on time for call time, rehearsals, and scheduled worship sets.
  • Arriving at least 30 minutes early allows us to prepare spiritually and musically as one team.

3. Rehearsals & Soundcheck

  • Attendance in rehearsals and soundcheck is essential to keep unity and flow in worship.
  • Kindly be well-prepared with your parts before the session, allowing us to focus our hearts and minds on worship.
  • Kindly follow the instructions of the worship leader or music director with a cooperative and humble attitude.

4. Dress Code

  • We kindly encourage everyone to wear modest and presentable clothing during worship, as a way of honoring God and creating a respectful atmosphere for worship.
  • Please avoid sleeveless tops, shorts, crop tops, or any attire that may cause distraction.

5. Worship Conduct

  • Let us give our undivided attention to God, setting aside distractions.

6. Team Unity & Respect

  • Let us honor and respect each member of the team, reflecting the love and humility of Christ.
  • A heart that worships God also honors people.
  • Unity within the team reflects God’s love and brings glory to His name.

7. Instrument & Equipment Care

  • Instruments and sound equipment play a vital role in leading others in worship, so let’s handle them with excellence, care, and respect.
  • For everyone’s safety and to maintain order, only designated personnel should operate technical equipment.
  • Please return borrowed instruments, microphones, or cables properly after use, if there are any.
  • In case of any damage or technical issue, please inform the proper person right away so it can be addressed promptly.

Final Encouragement

Let us always give our best — because we are serving and worshiping the Lord.

“As each one has received a gift, minister it to one another, as good stewards of the manifold grace of God.” — 1 Peter 4:10 NKJV

May everything we do — from preparation to every worship moment — be done with joy, humility, and excellence, always pointing people to Jesus and giving all the glory to God.
